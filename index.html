About Me

Engineering the future
of autonomous systems

I am a Master's student in Mechatronics at Hochschule Ravensburg-Weingarten, Germany, with a deep passion for robotics and autonomous systems. My work sits at the intersection of embedded computing, machine learning, and robot software.

My master's thesis — in collaboration with Olive Robotics — focuses on real-time IMU-based terrain classification using ensemble learning, achieving over 90% accuracy across multiple terrain types. I've also built systems ranging from vision-language navigation robots to industrial robotic arm path planning with MoveIt2.

Prior to my master's, I worked as a Werkstudent at Autecc GmbH on hexapod robotics and ROS2 workflows, and interned at Qualytech in India on industrial sensor integration. I bring a strong combination of hands-on hardware experience and software engineering skills.
